Synthesizer Jam Recorded Sessions

All music is recorded over a one to two hour period. Then cut up and processed utilizing some light mixing and added effects with Audacity.


Micheal Vawlter: Modular Synth / Guitar

Thane: Piano / Drums

Charles Stevenson: Modular Synth

This jam was recorded out of town at Thane/Michael’s studio. I had written a patch to get back into the flow of creating music on the modular synthesizer after dedicating much of the summer to learning music theory and piano. A lot of the inspiration came from a trip to upper Michigan, where I escaped the hectic pace of city life. On the last day of the trip, a violent thunderstorm caused a power surge, and the hum of generators that followed the blackout inspired me to start the track with a drone and build the song from there. The melody and harmony were influenced by the arpeggiated tune of Kakariko Village from Zelda, set in the key of Bflat.
This was our second time getting together. Once again, we decided to meet at Kerouac and perform in the side room. The goal was to continue building a community and invite other musicians to join in. It took about an hour to really get started, but once we found our groove, the music began to flow nicely. Unfortunately, someone, slightly inebriated, joined us on piano. While it sounded great at times, we didn’t capture that part of the recording. Overall, after this session, we realized the importance of doing a little preparation if we plan to jam in public again.
Our first jam session together was planned the previous November, emerging from a growing community called Modsquad in Columbus, Ohio. We had a great turnout, with some fascinating visuals provided by Ian’s modular visual rig. Overall, the event showed real potential for hosting public electronic music jam sessions.

Charles Stevenson
