Overview

HGRIC is the Human Genetics Research Informatics Core, housed in the biomedical research tower at The Ohio State University. It serves hundreds of researchers by maintaining a consortium database with more than thirty thousand patients. It processes millions of gene variants to look for a pattern in asymptomatic dilated cardiomyopathy.

Technology

We utilize a suite of all types of software akin to a full stack developer. The database is built on db2, a relational database, inserted in a framework known as BioComputing or BC for short. BC comes with a suite of triggers and a form system to help non-technical users enter in data on a day to day basis. However; when patient's progress through the study, HGRIC has built custom webportals using ag-grid and angular to highlight, show and describe information in the database. Typically, depending on what is being studied there are also Java classes that handle pipeline processes. This includes the download and processing of new genomic information, inventory reports of blood samples, and adjudication and generation of a patient's lab report.
