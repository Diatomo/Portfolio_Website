Overview

HGRIC is the Human Genetics Research Informatics Core, housed in the biomedical research tower at The Ohio State University. It serves hundreds of researchers by maintaining a consortium database with more than thirty thousand patients and millions of variants. It processes and adjudicates the prognosis of these variants searching for an asymptomatic pattern causing dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM).



Technology

We utilize a suite of all types of software akin to a full stack developer. The database is built on IBM's DB2, a relational database, inserted in a framework known as BioComputing (BC) for short. BC comes with a suite of triggers and a form system to help non-technical users enter in data on a day to day basis. However; when patient's progress through the study, HGRIC has built custom webportals using ag-grid and angular to highlight, show and describe information in the database. Typically, depending on what is being studied there are also Java classes that handle pipeline processes. This includes the download and processing of new genomic information, inventory reports of blood samples, and the adjudication and generation of a patient's genetic results.
